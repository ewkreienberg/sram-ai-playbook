Executive Advisory Brief

SRAM AI Adoption Executive Outline

Date: February 28, 2026 | Company: SRAM LLC (private; SRAM = Scott, Ray, and Sam)

Prepared by Donovan Palmer

1) Where SRAM Is Well Positioned for AI

  • SRAM has a large, structured support knowledge base (manuals, compatibility guides, troubleshooting, warranty guidance), which is strong AI input.
  • SRAM also has connected product workflows through AXS and Hammerhead. AXS is SRAM's wireless electronic shifting ecosystem and app experience; Hammerhead is SRAM's cycling computer platform for ride and device data.
  • Those connected interactions (setup, firmware updates, diagnostics, usage data) create practical data loops for better support and smarter recommendation workflows.

2) Target Processes, Pain Points, Tools, and Why Each Tool Fits

1) Rider/dealer support, warranty, compatibility help

Main pain point: Teams spend too much time searching documents and repeating standard troubleshooting steps.

AI tool stack: Zendesk AI + Amazon Kendra + Amazon Bedrock (Claude).

Why fit: Zendesk fits current ticket operations, Amazon Kendra finds approved content quickly, and Bedrock turns retrieved content into clear response drafts. All on AWS, consistent with SRAM's existing infrastructure.

2) Demand forecasting and inventory allocation

Main pain point: Forecast misses lead to stockouts, rush shipping, and markdown losses.

AI tool stack: AWS SageMaker.

Why fit: Strong for time-series forecasting and planner exception handling across multi-channel product portfolios.

3) Bicycle manufacturer proposal and spec package creation

Main pain point: Proposal cycles are slow and manual, delaying response and lowering win rates.

AI tool stack: Salesforce Einstein/Agentforce + CPQ features.

Why fit: Fits existing quoting and package-configuration workflow, improving speed and consistency.

4) Technical documentation and translation

Main pain point: Updates take too long to appear in manuals and localized content.

AI tool stack: Amazon Bedrock + DeepL API + workflow automation (Atlassian/ServiceNow).

Why fit: Accelerates draft + translation cycle while preserving review and approval controls.

5) Compatible next-part recommendations (Phase 3)

Main pain point: Customers often finish purchase/service without clear guidance on next compatible part.

AI tool stack: Compatibility rules engine + recommender + Bedrock explanation layer.

Why fit: Rules protect fit accuracy, recommendation model lifts basket value, and explanation layer improves trust.

6) Product development workflow support (Phase 3)

Main pain point: Engineering teams need faster translation of field issues into product requirements and quicker test planning cycles.

AI tool stack: Amazon Bedrock + issue/telemetry analytics + test-workflow automation.

Why fit: Supports requirements mining, firmware test acceleration, and design-review preparation without jumping to full design automation. Not included in Year-1 financial totals.

Rollout sequence

  • 0-90 days: support + compatibility assistant pilot with tight scope.
  • Start scope: AXS drivetrain + Hammerhead device support, dealer inbox + high-volume web forms, compatibility and firmware/update issues first.
  • Pilot decision rule: AI drafts responses; human agents approve all customer-facing responses.
  • 90-180 days: forecasting rollout for top product/channel groups.
  • 180-365 days: expand to bicycle manufacturer proposal support and multi-brand recommendation workflows.

Risk controls

  • Human approval for warranty and safety exceptions.
  • Compatibility answers sourced from approved documents only.
  • Weekly quality review with rollback trigger if quality drops for two consecutive weeks.

6) CEO Decision

  • Approve a focused 90-day Phase 1 pilot for support and compatibility.
  • Approve this exact starting scope: AXS + Hammerhead support workflows in dealer/support channels, with compatibility and firmware/update issues first.
  • Assign one accountable business owner and one cross-functional delivery team.
  • Maintain sequencing discipline: bounded, well-defined problems first; broader transformation after measured proof.
