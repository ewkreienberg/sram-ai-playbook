## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|

| 1991 | Grip Shift extended to mountain bikes. |
| 2002 | Acquired **RockShox** (MTB suspension). |

## 7. THREAT ASSESSMENT

### 7.1 How Aggressive Is SRAM in the Market?

- **Very aggressive in MTB**: Eagle (1x) has effectively become the market standard; Shimano XTR is the only credible competitor
- **Aggressive in road/gravel**: 2024 Red AXS E1 + WorldTour parity (2026) demonstrates intent to challenge Shimano's historic road dominance
- **Growing in e-bike**: Eagle Powertrain expanding OEM list; this is a long-term threat to Bosch/Shimano EP dominance
- **Digital ecosystem play**: Hammerhead Karoo 3 + AXS Web = attempt to lock in data layer as cycling computers become performance tools
- **Acquisition velocity**: Hammerhead (2022), Velocio (2022), TIME (2021), Ochain (2025) = 1 acquisition per year average → portfolio will keep expanding

---

### 7.2 What It Would Take to Compete Effectively

| Axis | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **MTB drivetrain** | Must match T-Type durability and zero-adjust reliability OR compete on price at 2–3x lower cost |
| **Wireless ecosystem** | Open protocol or deep interoperability advantage vs. SRAM's closed AXS |
| **Service/warranty** | Superior customer service is a genuine white-space opportunity (SRAM's clear weakness) |
| **Road** | Shimano 105 Di2 ($800 street price) undercuts SRAM Rival AXS on value significantly |
| **E-bike** | Must own motor technology (not rely on 3rd party) to compete with SRAM's integrated Eagle Powertrain vision |
